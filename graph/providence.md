# Providence

## Summary

Marcus Aurelius presents providence as one of three possible cosmic orders alongside necessity and random confusion. He counsels that if providence governs the universe, one should make oneself worthy of divine help, while even if chaos rules, one's ruling intelligence remains untouched by external tempests. In contemplating what befalls us, we should consider whether events happen by chance or according to providence, neither blaming the former nor accusing the latter.
