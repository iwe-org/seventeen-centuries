# Self-renunciation-morality

## Summary

Self-renunciation morality encompasses the sentiments of surrender, sacrifice for one's neighbour, and acting "for others" rather than for oneself. Nietzsche argues this morality must be brought to judgment and viewed with suspicion, as its pleasantness to both the giver and receiver is no argument in its favor. He suggests these altruistic sentiments may be deceptions disguised by "witchery and sugar."
