# Concealment

## Summary

Concealment characterizes the condition of the philosophical recluse, whose ideas acquire a twilight-colour and something uncommunicative through prolonged solitude. Books themselves are written precisely to hide what is within us, and the recluse doubts whether a philosopher can have ultimate opinions at all. Every philosophy conceals another philosophy beneath it; every opinion is a lurking-place, and every word is also a mask.
