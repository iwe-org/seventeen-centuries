# Hobbes

## Summary

Hobbes appears as the "genuine Englishman" who condemned laughter as "a bad infirmity of human nature, which every thinking mind will strive to overcome." Nietzsche positions Hobbes as a foil to his own philosophy, which instead ranks philosophers by the quality of their laughter and suggests that even gods cannot refrain from laughing at serious matters.
