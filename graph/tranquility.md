# Tranquility

## Summary

Tranquility arises from following reason in all things, allowing one to be simultaneously calm and active, cheerful and collected. It is achieved not by avoiding external circumstances but by letting one's judgement about them rest undisturbed. When we cease our mental pursuits and aversions toward things, they naturally remain quiet, and inner peace follows.
