# German depth

## Summary

Nietzsche questions whether the celebrated notion of German depth is actually something different and worse than genuine profundity. He suggests it reflects the German soul's manifold, disordered nature---its love of clouds, obscurity, and everything uncertain and undeveloped. The Germans embrace things as "deep" simply because they are crepuscular and shrouded, confusing their difficult digestion of experience with profundity itself.
