# Gods

## Summary

In Meditations, Marcus Aurelius presents the gods as benevolent powers who have arranged all things well for mankind and can cooperate with those who pray properly. He argues one should pray not for external outcomes but for inner transformation, and that the gods' existence is comprehended through the constant experience of their power. Nietzsche offers a contrasting view in Beyond Good and Evil, suggesting that gods philosophize with golden laughter and cannot refrain from ridicule even in holy matters.
