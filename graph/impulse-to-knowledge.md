# Impulse to knowledge

## Summary

Nietzsche questions whether the "impulse to knowledge" is truly the driving force behind philosophy. He argues that other fundamental impulses use knowledge merely as an instrument, with each impulse attempting to establish itself as the ultimate end of existence. While scholars may possess a genuine, small "impulse to knowledge" that works independently like clockwork, philosophers are driven by deeper moral purposes that reveal their true nature.
