# Tempter-god

## Summary

The tempter-god refers to Dionysus, whom Nietzsche calls the great equivocator and "born rat-catcher of consciences." This divinity possesses the genius of the heart: the ability to descend into every soul, impose silence on the self-conceited, and send seekers away richer in themselves. Dionysus philosophizes with fearless honesty, expressing love for mankind while desiring to make humanity stronger, more evil, and more profound.
