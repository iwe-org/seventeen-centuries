# Divine justice

## Summary

Nietzsche characterizes the Jewish Old Testament as the book of divine justice, containing men, things, and sayings on an immense scale unmatched by Greek or Indian literature. He expresses reverence before these stupendous remains of what humanity once was, and suggests that the taste for the Old Testament serves as a touchstone for distinguishing between great and small souls.
