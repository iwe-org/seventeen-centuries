# Tacitus

## Summary

Nietzsche invokes Tacitus as representative of the ancient world's view that the Jews were "a people born for slavery." This characterization serves as a foil against the Jews' own self-designation as "the chosen people," highlighting the radical inversion of values that Nietzsche identifies as the origin of the slave-insurrection in morals.
