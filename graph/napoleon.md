# Napoleon

## Summary

Napoleon represents for Nietzsche the exemplary absolute ruler whose appearance brought "higher happiness" to a Europe weary of herd morality and weak leadership. He is counted among the great synthesizers—alongside Goethe, Beethoven, and Schopenhauer—who anticipated the "European of the future" and transcended narrow nationalism. His encounter with Goethe, exclaiming "voilà un homme!", reveals his capacity to recognize genuine human greatness where others expected only the "gentle, good-hearted, weak-willed" German stereotype.
