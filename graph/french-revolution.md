# French Revolution

## Summary

The French Revolution is characterized as a "terrible farce" that appeared superfluous when examined closely, yet noble spectators throughout Europe projected their own indignation and enthusiasm onto it from a distance. This passionate interpretation by outsiders eventually obscured the actual events, demonstrating how posterity can misunderstand and romanticize the past to make it more palatable.
