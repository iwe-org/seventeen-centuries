# Moral sentiment

## Summary

Moral sentiment refers to the subtle, refined, and diverse feelings about moral worth that have developed in European culture. Nietzsche contrasts this sophisticated moral sensitivity with the clumsy, presumptuous attempts by philosophers to create a "Science of Morals," arguing that moral sentiment far exceeds in nuance what any systematic moral philosophy has yet achieved.
