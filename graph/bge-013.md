# 13

Psychologists should bethink themselves before putting down the instinct of [Self-preservation](self-preservation.md) as the cardinal instinct of an organic being. A living thing seeks above all to *discharge* its strength---life itself is *[Will to Power](will-to-power.md)*; self-preservation is only one of the indirect and most frequent *results* thereof. In short, here, as everywhere else, let us beware of *superfluous* [Teleological principles](teleological-principles.md)!---one of which is the instinct of self-preservation (we owe it to [Spinoza](spinoza.md)'s inconsistency). It is thus, in effect, that method ordains, which must be essentially economy of principles.
