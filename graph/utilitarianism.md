# Utilitarianism

## Summary

Nietzsche critiques utilitarianism as sharing its origins with Socratic morality, both stemming from a common mentality that equates "good" with "useful and pleasant" and views evil merely as stupidity with unpleasant consequences. This mode of reasoning is characterized as belonging to the populace, who judge wrongdoing only by its disagreeable results rather than by any deeper moral consideration.
