# Cave

## Summary

The cave serves as Nietzsche's metaphor for the hidden depths of the philosopher's soul, where the recluse sits alone in discourse with himself, becoming like a cave-bear or dragon guarding treasure. Behind every cave lies a still deeper cave, an abyss beneath every foundation, suggesting that all philosophy is merely "foreground philosophy" and every expressed opinion conceals deeper, unexpressed truths.
