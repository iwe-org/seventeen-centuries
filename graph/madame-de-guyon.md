# Madame de Guyon

## Summary

Nietzsche cites Madame de Guyon as an example of a feminine, sensual form of religious passion that unconsciously longs for a mystical and physical union with the divine. Her spiritual devotion represents the tender, modest side of the passion for God, in contrast to the churlish fervor of Luther or the slavish exaltation of Augustine.
