# Conviction

## Summary

In philosophy, conviction refers to the underlying personal belief that eventually surfaces in every philosopher's work. Nietzsche suggests that no matter how rational or objective a philosophy may appear, there comes a point where the philosopher's own conviction—their deeper, perhaps irrational commitment—reveals itself, arriving like "the ass, beautiful and most strong" from the ancient mysteries.
