# 33

It cannot be helped: the sentiment of surrender, of sacrifice for one's neighbour, and all [Self-renunciation-morality](self-renunciation-morality.md), must be mercilessly called to account, and brought to judgment; just as the aesthetics of "[Disinterested contemplation](disinterested-contemplation.md)," under which the emasculation of art nowadays seeks insidiously enough to create itself a good conscience. There is far too much witchery and sugar in the sentiments "for others" and "*not* for myself," for one not needing to be doubly distrustful here, and for one asking promptly: "Are they not perhaps---*deceptions*?"---That they *please*---him who has them, and him who enjoys their fruit, and also the mere spectator---that is still no argument in their *favour*, but just calls for caution. Let us therefore be cautious!
