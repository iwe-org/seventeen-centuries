# Galiani

## Summary

Nietzsche cites Galiani alongside Hamlet as an example of "free insolent minds" who use cynicism to conceal their broken, proud, and incurable hearts. These figures employ folly itself as a mask for their unfortunate over-assured knowledge, representing one of the refined disguises that profound sufferers adopt to protect themselves from sympathizing hands.
