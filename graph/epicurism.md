# Epicurism

## Summary

Nietzsche presents Epicurism as one of the refined disguises adopted by those who have suffered profoundly. Rather than a genuine philosophical stance, it serves as a mask of ostentatious gaiety and lightness toward suffering, allowing the deeply wounded to deflect sympathizing hands and protect themselves from those who cannot understand their experience. The Epicurist posture of taking suffering lightly is thus a deliberate strategy of the noble sufferer who wishes to be misunderstood.
