# Causality

## Summary

Nietzsche argues that we should not assume multiple kinds of causality when a single one might suffice. He proposes that our belief in causality is fundamentally rooted in our belief in the causality of the will---that will can only operate on will. This leads to the hypothesis that all mechanical action and active force might ultimately be reducible to the Will to Power as the sole form of causality.
