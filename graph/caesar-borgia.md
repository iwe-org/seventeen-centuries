# Caesar Borgia

## Summary

Caesar Borgia serves as Nietzsche's prime example of the "man of prey" - a figure of natural vitality and health whom conventional moralists wrongly condemn as morbid or corrupt. Nietzsche argues that such "tropical men" represent the healthiest specimens of humanity, misunderstood by those who favor mediocrity and the "temperate zones" of moral timidity.
