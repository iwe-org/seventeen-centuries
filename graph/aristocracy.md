# Aristocracy

## Summary

A healthy aristocracy views itself not as a function of kingship or commonwealth, but as society's highest justification and significance. It accepts the sacrifice of the masses as necessary scaffolding for the elevation of a select class to higher existence. Within such an organization, individuals may treat each other as equals, but the aristocracy as a whole must embody the Will to Power, striving to grow and gain ascendancy over other bodies.
