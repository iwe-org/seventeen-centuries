# Liberation

## Summary

Liberation is characterized as a voluptuous distance and remoteness, likened to a bird that flies ever higher to see more beneath it. However, Nietzsche warns against clinging to one's own liberation, identifying it as a danger for the free spirit. True independence requires not becoming attached even to freedom itself, as such attachment can become another form of bondage.
