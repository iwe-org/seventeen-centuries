# Christian sentiments

## Summary

Christian sentiments are presented as a form of moral pressure that functioned like an intercalary period of restraint and fasting in European history. Under this pressure, impulses learned to humble and submit themselves while simultaneously being purified and sharpened. It was precisely during the most Christian period of European history, under the constraint of these sentiments, that the sexual impulse sublimated into romantic love.
