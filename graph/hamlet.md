# Hamlet

## Summary

Nietzsche invokes Hamlet as an exemplar of cynicism that serves as a mask for profound suffering. Like other free insolent minds, Hamlet conceals a broken, proud, and incurable heart beneath his bitter wit. His case illustrates how the deeply wounded adopt various disguises to protect themselves from contact with those who have not known their depths of suffering.
